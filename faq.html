Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about independent living, accessibility, and how to get involved.

General Questions

What is independent living?

Independent living means having the freedom to make your own choices and live in your community with access to the support and resources you need.

Who benefits from independent living services?

Individuals with disabilities of all ages can benefit from services that promote independence, skill-building, and community involvement.

What kind of services are available?

Services may include peer support, life skills training, advocacy, transportation assistance, and access to community resources.

Accessibility & Inclusion

What does accessibility mean?

Accessibility means creating environments where everyone, regardless of ability, can participate fully and independently.

How can I make my environment more accessible?

You can start by removing physical barriers, improving communication, and being mindful of inclusive practices in everyday situations.

Getting Involved

How can I support independent living?

You can support by volunteering, raising awareness, sharing resources, and advocating for accessibility in your community.

Do I need experience to get involved?

No experience is necessary. Anyone can make a difference by learning, supporting, and being involved in their community.
