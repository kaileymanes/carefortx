Independent Living Guide

Learn what independent living means, why it matters, and how individuals can build a life with dignity, choice, and opportunity.

What is Independent Living?

Independent living is the ability for individuals with disabilities to live in their communities with the same choices, control, and opportunities as anyone else. It does not mean doing everything alone — it means having access to the support and resources needed to live a self-directed life.

Why It Matters

Everyone deserves the freedom to make decisions about their own life. Independent living supports dignity, inclusion, and participation in the community. It allows individuals to pursue education, careers, relationships, and personal goals without unnecessary barriers.

Accessibility Barriers

Limited access to buildings, transportation, and services can make everyday tasks more difficult.

Employment Gaps

Finding inclusive workplaces and equal job opportunities remains a challenge for many individuals.

Limited Resources

Not everyone has access to the support services needed to live independently.

Tips for Independent Living

Here are a few ways individuals can build and maintain independence:

  • Connect with local organizations and support networks
  • Explore assistive technology and accessibility tools
  • Develop daily living and financial management skills
  • Advocate for your needs and rights
  • Build a strong community of support

Support Systems

Independent living is supported by a network of organizations, advocates, and community members. Centers for Independent Living provide services such as peer support, skills training, advocacy, and resource referrals.
